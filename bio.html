About Me

I am a Columbus native who grew up fascinated by technology. From obsessively fighting my way through Nintendo games to writing stories on my Apple II which my cousins voraciously consumed, I spent much of my childhood in front of a screen. In high school I taught myself some limited Visual Basic and Java but struggled to find helpful resources on this.

When I started college, I excitedly declared a major in Computer Information Technologies, but quickly found that I already knew the things they were covering. In my youthful impatience I switched majors and have regretted it ever since. I convinced myself it was too late for me to go back and learn software development, though I often dreamt of it.

Finally, I had enough of boring jobs that neither challenged me nor made use of my skills and decided to enroll in WeCanCodeIT and get back on the path I have wanted to walk all this time. There, I learned to use TDD and scrum to become a full stack Java developer. I am now looking forward to getting started using my skills to provide for my wife and our adorably obese cats.
